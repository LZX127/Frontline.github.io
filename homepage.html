What is ‘Infodemic’ and what makes it a big problem?

The World Health Organization (WHO) coined the term Infodemic, a combination of the words “information” and “pandemic” to describe our current world situation characterized by “an overabundance of information – some accurate and some not – that makes it hard for people to find trustworthy sources and reliable guidance when they need it.”

The coronavirus, a global pandemic, and the infodemic have similarities, they both spread quickly and widely. However, it is noteworthy that the proliferation of disinformation and misinformation spread through social media and not only threaten and undermine democratic principles but also challenges institutions and the foundations they are built on.

As the world continues to cope with the coronavirus pandemic, we are also under the threat of a likewise dangerous outbreak of inaccurate, false, and misleading information. Amidst this ‘infodemic’, In order to fight the spread of false information, it means prevention to avoid oneself being deceived. Moreover, it is best that you know the different ways you can protect yourself from the spread of information disorder.
